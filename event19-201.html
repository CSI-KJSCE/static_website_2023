Events Organized in 2019-20

Study Abroad Seminar
It was conducted on 28th August 2019 by the speaker - Mrs. Reema Chitalia. The seminar aimed at clarifying all the doubts regarding studying abroad. Mrs. Reema Chitalia also shared her journey of Master's degree abroad. The seminar was open to all the students of K.J. Somaiya College of Engineering.
TechRoutes
It was conducted on the 30th of August 2019. This seminar was conducted for the FEs to provide them with an overview of the various domains of study that they can explore throughout their four years of engineering and how they can further create a career for themselves in that particular domain. The speakers for the same were Technical Head Kaustubh Damania, General Secretary Aditya Panchal, and Jt. General Secretary Varada Harikumar while PRO Preeti Poddar hosted the event.
NodeJS Workshop
It was conducted on 5th September 2019. This workshop was conducted for SEs and TEs. The participants developed their File Manager during the workshop from scratch. The speakers for the workshop were Technical Head - Kaustubh Damania and Jt. Technical Head - Zenil Haria
Game Development Using Python
It was conducted on 16th September 2019. This workshop was conducted for FE’s. The participants developed games from scratch using the Pygame module by applying real-life physics and maths concepts. The speakers for the same were Technical Heads - Kaustubh Damania and Zenil Haria, Technical Team - Vinit Mundra, Talha Chafekar and Saurav Joshi, Event Head - Smit Vora, Event Team - Tushar Bapecha and Rugved Bongale, Public Relations Officer - Preeti Poddar and Public Relations Team - Aafiya Hussain, Jt. General Secretary - Varada Harikumar, Creative Team - Hiral Sheth and Sakshi Pandey, General Secretary - Aditya Panchal, and Secretary - Darshan Chheda.
Machine Learning Seminar
It was conducted on 23rd September 2019. This seminar was conducted only for the FEs to introduce them to the notion of Machine Learning and its implementation in the field of Artificial Intelligence. The three models under Machine Learning were the main point of discussion. The speakers for the same were General Secretary Aditya Panchal, Jt. General Secretary Varada Harikumar, Public Relations Officer Preeti Poddar, Financial Secretary Mittal Jain, and Secretary Darshan Chheda.
Machine Learning Workshop
It was conducted on the 24th of September 2019. This workshop was in continuation to the seminar on Machine Learning. It aimed at putting the concepts of Machine Learning to practice. The speakers for the same were Secretary Darshan Chheda and Technical Team member Saurav Joshi.
Abhiyantriki Events
It was held on 12TH October 2019. The events were: Code in X 3.0 and Bit Rebels. Both the events challenged the participant's adaptability, technical skills, and management skills, thus grooming an all around performance from the coders.
ReactJS Workshop
It was conducted on the 23rd and 24th of October 2019 in collaboration with Facebook Developer Circles, Mumbai. This workshop was open for FE, SE, and TEs. The workshop aimed at exploring ReactJS as a frontend technology. By the end of Day 2, the participants had developed an app from scratch. The speakers for the workshop were: General Secretary Aditya Panchal, Jt. General Secretary Varada Harikumar, Technical Heads Kaustubh Damania, and Zenil Haria, Event Team member Rugved Bongale, Technical Team member Vinit Mundra and Secretary Darshan Chheda.
HackIt 2.0
HackIt 2.0 (Technext 2020) was the second edition of HackIt which was a full-fledged hackathon where the participants worked on five primary domains – Artificial Intelligence/ Machine Learning, Internet of Things (IoT), Blockchain, Augmented/Virtual Reality (AR/VR) and Fintech.
Code Wars 4.0
It was a inter-departmental competitive programming contest that focused on enhancing healthy competition between the various departments of KJSCE. It was a 24-hours contest which started on 5 April 2020, 9pm and ended on 6 April 2020, 9pm. The aim of this competition was to not just put forward challenging and logical problems, but also to build an environment of competitive programming in our college.
